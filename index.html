About

I care about the link between environmental protection and population health. I’m especially interested in research that partners with communities to design interventions that are feasible, respectful, and durable.

Research identity

Environmental health and infectious disease epidemiology with a global health lens, grounded in real-world implementation and policy relevance.

Teaching & mentorship

I enjoy helping students build practical skills—data literacy, study design, and clear scientific communication.

Life outdoors

Mountains are my compass: trail running, cycling, and climbing keep me curious, humble, and committed to protecting the places that restore us.

Research

Broadly: how environmental exposures and social conditions shape health, and how we can prevent harm—especially where resources are limited.

Mountains & nature

I’m happiest moving through alpine landscapes—trail running, cycling, and climbing. I see outdoor joy and environmental protection as the same story: stewardship.

How the outdoors shapes my work

  • Long-term thinking: ecosystems and health systems both require patience.
  • Risk awareness: humility around uncertainty, exposure, and safety.
  • Community: partnerships matter—on a rope team and in public health.

Where I show up
